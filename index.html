About Joey

My friends call me Joe. I live in beautiful Brooklyn.
Joey is the collection of my work, collaborations, and concepts.

+ I began designing and developing 13 years ago. It started when I hacked my very own blog before blogs were blogs. I received my Bachelor's degree from Virginia Commonwealth University studying Computer Science and Mass Communications with a focus on Creative Advertising. I work in the field as an on-site designer/developer for David's Bridal and Joey. I've furthered my education by taking classes at Third Ward Art & Design Center and will continue by participating in the School of Visual Arts Interaction Design Summer Intensive.

Tambaran

Tambaran is one of NYC's oldest Tribal Art galleries specializing in exceptionally beautiful museum quality pieces.

With designer Joseph Mueller and developer Efrat Weidberg we delivered a logo refresh, a website built from outlined information with fully functional Joomla-powered content management and a private buyer login for custom private galleries showcasing public and non-public work.

Monthly emails are designed and distributed to their private email list and bi-monthly visits are made for tech support.

Deliverables:

  • branding
  • web
  • email
  • cms

David's Bridal & OurWeddingDay

Responsible for the look and feel of hundreds of email blasts, all web/print ads, web/email features and landing pages for the country's largest bridal retail store and its sister site, OurWeddingDay.

Redesigned corporate styleguide, website, branding, logged-in vendor control panel, and subsequent content pages. Directed Symbio, back-end developers located in China until the company expanded and the designer/developer role became more specialized.

Snowden River Liquors

A local neighborhood store committed to providing customers a world class selection of spirits, wine and beers with a hometown touch.

With co-developer Efrat Weidberg we designed and developed a user-friendly combination mobile/desktop site based on the Tumblr CMS.

With it running on the Tumblr engine management at the liquor store can take a picture of a new arrival with an iOS/Android based device and create a new entry without having to leave the counter to type up review on a desktop.
